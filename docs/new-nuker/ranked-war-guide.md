# Ranked War Guide

Ranked Wars (RWs) are one of the most important competitive events in Torn. Preparing properly and understanding your role during the war can make the difference between a win and a loss — and directly affects the rewards your entire faction receives.

:::info[What is a Ranked War?]

A Ranked War is a **"Tug of War"** style fight between two factions. Each side attacks the other to gain **score**, with the goal of crossing a target net score first. Score is equivalent to respect earned per hit — so how you attack matters.

- The target score is based on both factions' rank and member counts
- If neither faction reaches the target within 24 hours, it decreases by **1% per hour**
- **Mugging** only yields **75%** of the score that hospitalizing/leaving does
- After the war, both factions receive bonus respect, points, and caches based on rank, participation, member count, and win/loss conditions

:::

---

## Pre-War Checklist

:::warning[Do these before the war starts]

Complete every item on this list **before** the war begins. Failing to prepare is one of the most common ways members hurt their faction's performance.

:::

| Task                                   | Notes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      |
| -------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Turn revives OFF**                   | Set revives to "No One" in settings. Only enable them the moment you need a revive, then disable immediately after. Leaving them on lets the opponent hostile-revive and farm you.                                                                                         |
| **Stack energy & rehab**               | Start stacking at least 48 hours out in case of an OD. Rehab after fully stacking to avoid a battle stat debuff during the war.                                                                                                                                            |
| **Clear medical & booster cooldowns**  | You'll burn through blood bags and FHCs fast. Having full cooldowns available at the start is critical.                                                                                                                                                                    |
| **Take Vicodin ~2–3 hours before war** | Gives +25% battle stats for 4–6 hours. Only do this if you have no drug cooldown — Vicodin OD only costs 150 happiness so the risk is low.                                                                                                                                 |
| **Pick targets & add to YATA**         | A battle stat spy list may be provided before the war. Save targets in [YATA](https://yata.yt) so you can hit them quickly. Aim for targets with at least **75% of your total battle stats** for max Fair Fight bonus. TornTools + TornStats can show FF bonus per target. |
| **Install recommended scripts**        | [How to request revives](/reviving/requesting-revives) — all methods for requesting revives during the war. [Chain Watcher script](https://greasyfork.org/en/scripts/381435-chain-watcher) — audio alert when chain timer runs low.                                        |
| **Stock up on items**                  | Buy temporary items (FHCs, Xanax, cans), blood bags, and anything else you need **beforehand**. There won't be time during the war.                                                                                                                                        |
| **Set up loadouts**                    | Pre-configure up to 3 loadouts in your inventory so you can swap weapons/armor/temps quickly mid-war.                                                                                                                                                                      |
| **Check attack settings**              | Make sure your attack settings match your playstyle and build. See [this Torn forum guide](https://www.torn.com/forums.php#/p=threads&f=61&t=16252083&b=0&a=0&to=21979427) for recommendations.                                                                            |
| **Do NOT open parcels**                | Opponents will send booby-trapped parcels (disguised as FHCs, medical items, etc.). Ignore all parcels received shortly before or during a war.                                                                                                                            |

## During the War

### Attacking Strategy

The goal is to maximize your score contribution while minimizing how much the opponent can score off you.

:::tip[Key Score Multipliers]

- **Fair Fight (FF):** Ranges from 1x–3x based on stat similarity. Target players with ≥75% of your battle stats for a **3x FF bonus**.
- **Retaliation bonus:** Hospitalizing someone who attacked a faction member in the last 5 minutes gives a **1.5x multiplier**. Always prioritize retals over normal hits.
- **Chain bonus:** Large chunks of bonus score are awarded at chain milestones. All chain bonus hits should be on opponent faction members.

:::

| Target Type                    | What to Do       | Why                                                         |
| ------------------------------ | ---------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Online / high stat players** | Hospitalize them | Forces medical cooldown, keeps them out of the fight longer |
| **Offline / low stat players** | Leave them       | Short hospital time = you can keep farming them for respect |

### Chaining

Chaining is **the single most important activity** during a ranked war. The chain multiplier dramatically increases respect (and score) per hit.

- Keep the chain alive **at all costs** — even if it means hitting someone outside the opponent faction
- Every chain bonus hit should land on an opponent faction member
- Open multiple browser tabs for fast attacks — you may get hit the moment you leave hospital
- If the chain is about to drop and you can't hit anyone, make an **outside hit** on any target to save it

:::danger[Chain drops lose wars]

A dropped chain resets your multiplier and hands the opponent a massive advantage. Monitor the chain timer constantly — the Chain Watcher script will alert you audibly.

:::

### Staying Safe When Not Active

If you're going AFK, sleeping, or being farmed, **do not just log off**. The opponent will farm you for easy respect.

<details>
<summary>Ways to self-hospitalize</summary>

| Method                           | Duration   | Notes                                                                                                                                                                |
| -------------------------------- | ---------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Wrong blood bag                  | ~1 hour    | Great for short offline windows. Let energy build, make a hit or two, then drop yourself. Repeatable without burning medical cooldown if you're offline for 30+ min. |
| Small Explosive Device (SED)     | ~5 hours   | Buy an SED, put it in a box, open it. Reliable but expensive.                                                                                                        |
| Failed crime                     | 8–9+ hours | Long hospital time but costs crime XP and **requires a revive to get out** — wrecks your medical cooldown if you don't.                                              |
| Outside hit (take a loss)        | 4–6 hours  | Depends on who hits you and their perks.                                                                                                                             |
| Ask a faction member to hosp you | Variable   | Someone with hospitalization merits can keep you in longer. Setting a bounty on yourself may also help.                                                              |

</details>

:::warning[Revives OFF before going offline]

Always make sure your revives are set to **No One** before you go offline or hospitalize yourself. An opponent can hostile-revive you and immediately start farming you.

:::

### If You're Going Overseas

Traveling overseas is a valid way to protect yourself for extended periods. You cannot be attacked while **in the air**, but you can be attacked once you've landed — however, opponents are unlikely to follow you to a far destination, so staying abroad at a distant country is often safe for AFK periods.

- Good destinations for **long-duration travel:** South Africa, UAE, China, Japan — the further the better for discouraging pursuit
- You can still **make hits while overseas** to help keep the chain alive — open a faction tab before flying
- This is ideal if you're being actively farmed or won't be around for several hours

### Chain Watching

Everyone is responsible for watching the chain — not just leadership.

- Keep some energy in reserve during low-activity hours (TCT night/morning) so you can make an emergency hit
- If you can't hit the opponent, hit anyone outside — chain survival comes first
- Communicate in faction chat and Discord if you see the timer dropping

### Turtling

When heavy hitters are offline and the opponent is dominating, the strategy shifts to **turtling**: staying hospitalized or overseas so the opponent can't farm your faction for score.

- Stay hospitalized with revives off
- Do minimal hits (just enough to maintain chain) while denying the opponent easy targets
- Leadership will call for turtling when needed — follow instructions in faction chat and Discord

### Coordinated Pushes & Group Attacks

Leadership may organize **pushes** to break a standstill or the opponent's chain:

- A Discord ping / faction mail will give a specific time
- Pre-open browser tabs with your targets loaded
- When the time hits: med out or get revived, then immediately attack your targets — online opponents first, then idle/offline
- The goal is to put the entire opponent faction in hospital simultaneously to turn the tide

**Group attacks** are rarer but may be organized for high-stat targets:

- Multiple members attack the same target simultaneously
- Spam temps and exploit the **distraction bonus** to take down players far above your stats

### Low Battle Stats / Can't Hit Anyone?

You can still contribute significantly even if your stats aren't competitive:

1. **Turn off revives and self-hospitalize** — deny the opponent free score
2. **Watch the chain** — make outside hits to keep it alive (don't hit during bonus windows)
3. **Assist group attacks** — join another member's fight and throw temps for the distraction bonus
4. **Stay engaged in comms** — let leadership know your situation

## Score Mechanics Reference

| Mechanic                     | Effect                                                 |
| ---------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ |
| Hospitalize / Leave          | Full respect score                                     |
| Mug                          | 75% of normal score                                    |
| Fair Fight (FF) max          | 3x multiplier (target has ≥75% of your stats)          |
| Retaliation hit              | 1.5x multiplier                                        |
| Chain multiplier             | Scales with chain length — higher = more score per hit |
| Chain bonus hits             | Large score dumps at milestone chain counts            |
| Hostile revive (by opponent) | They farm score off you — why revives must stay OFF    |

## Recommended Gear

<details>
<summary>Armor recommendations</summary>

| Slot         | Recommendation      | Notes                                                                                  |
| ------------ | ------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Helmet       | Combat Helmet       | Good armor rating — does **not** protect vs pepper spray. Skip if you train dexterity. |
| Helmet (alt) | Motorcycle Helmet   | Worse armor rating but protects vs pepper spray. Choose if you train dexterity.        |
| Chest        | Flexible Body Armor | Best armor rating — does **not** protect the neck.                                     |
| Chest (alt)  | Full Body Armor     | Slightly lower rating but protects the neck and is widely available in the armory.     |
| Hands        | Combat Gloves       | Solid armor rating.                                                                    |
| Legs         | Combat Pants        | Solid armor rating.                                                                    |
| Feet         | Combat Boots        | Solid armor rating.                                                                    |

_Note: These are general suggestions for newer players with lower networths. Higher stat and higher networth players may want to explorer using RW gear._

</details>

<details>
<summary>Weapon recommendations</summary>

| Type      | Option               | Strength                                    | Weakness                      |
| --------- | -------------------- | ------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------- |
| Melee     | Macana               | More accurate                               | Less damage                   |
| Melee     | Diamond Bladed Knife | More damage                                 | Less accurate                 |
| Primary   | ArmaLite             | Highest damage, accepts mods & special ammo | Less accurate                 |
| Secondary | Blowgun              | Poison DoT can deal massive damage          | Poison doesn't always trigger |

**Melee choice:** Use Macana if your Strength > Speed. Use DBK if your Speed > Strength.

_Note: These are general suggestions for newer players with lower networths. Higher stat and higher networth players may want to explorer using RW gear._

</details>
